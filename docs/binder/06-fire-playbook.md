# Fire Playbook — When Something Goes Wrong on the Road

> Franchise Binder §6 · drafted 2026-07-22 · reader: the dispatcher, mid-fire, phone in hand. Categories, not scripts — special-needs transport means anything is possible. Operator voice notes refine these over time.

## ACCIDENT (the legal one — timers are contractual, FA TPA §5a)
1. **Safety first:** driver confirms every child is safe; 911 if anyone is hurt or vehicles are disabled.
2. **Notify FirstAlt immediately** — not after the dust settles. Same call/channel you use for ride issues; then Brandon by email so it's in writing.
3. **Driver's written statement within 24 hours.** Call the driver, get the story while it's fresh, have them write it (their language is fine — translate it) — what happened, where, who was in the car, photos if any.
4. **1-hour response SLA:** any request FirstAlt makes about the accident must be answered within one hour until it's closed.
5. Notify the parent/guardian per the school's protocol (FA usually leads this — follow their instruction).
6. Log everything with timestamps. If the vehicle is out, treat the driver's remaining rides as a CALLOUT (below).

## BEHAVIORAL (tantrum, unsafe child behavior)
1. Driver pulls over safely; child stays in the vehicle unless unsafe.
2. Driver calls dispatch; dispatch conferences the school or guardian — the driver does not discipline, restrain, or negotiate.
3. If the ride can't continue, FA/the school decides the next step; we don't abandon a route mid-run without their sign-off.
4. Log it. Repeated behavior on a route = flag to the partner in writing (protects the driver and the pay).

## DRIVER NO-SHOW / GHOSTED
1. The monitor has usually flagged this before you (no accept by the window = assume ghosted).
2. **Cover first, lecture later.** Ping the ride's standing backup; no backup responding → next-nearest reliable driver (assignment tool ranks them).
3. If nobody can cover, tell FirstAlt before the pickup time, not after — a warned miss is survivable, a silent one isn't (§6c/§1b).
4. Afterward: the ghost gets the accountability call, and the ghosting is logged (feeds their reliability tier — chronic tier changes how the system treats them).

## RUNNING LATE / TRAFFIC
1. Driver tells dispatch the moment late looks likely — the app timeline is watched by FA, so we narrate before they ask.
2. Dispatch informs the school/parent per protocol. No speeding to make up time, ever — the app tracks it and a ticket costs more than a late arrival.

## CALLOUT (driver sick / can't run)
1. Thank them for the notice, then open the coverage tool: standing backup first, then the suggested swap chain (move driver A to cover, hand A's ride to B — never more than two moves).
2. Confirm the chain with each driver by call, not just SMS, for same-morning changes.
3. After it's resolved, one tap: should this cover become the standing backup for the ride?

## PARTNER COMPLAINT (FA §1b — "any complaint" can disqualify a driver, FA is sole judge)
1. Acknowledge to the partner same day, in writing; get specifics (date, ride, what was reported).
2. Get the driver's side within 24 hours, written down.
3. Respond with facts + corrective step. Never argue liability in writing without Malik seeing it first.
4. If FA pulls the driver, treat their routes as callouts and start coverage immediately.

## The three universal rules
1. **Kids safe → partner informed → coverage found → paperwork.** In that order, every time.
2. Anything involving injury, police, insurance, or a district goes to Malik the same hour.
3. Every fire gets logged when it's over — the log is what turns fires into playbook edits.
