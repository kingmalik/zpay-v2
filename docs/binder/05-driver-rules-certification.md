# Driver Rules & Certification Course — Master Content (EN source)

> Franchise Binder §5 · drafted 2026-07-22. This is the SOURCE text for the S7 trilingual certification course (EN/AM/AR + audio). Translation flow: Amharic blessed by Zubeda; Arabic needs a second full speaker for QA before ship. No driver takes a first ride until certified; recertification on any rule change.

## Module 1 — The job in one minute
You drive children with special needs to and from school. The children's safety is the entire job; the driving is second. The partner's app watches every ride — following the app rules is what makes sure you get paid.

## Module 2 — The six driving rules
1. **Accept on time.** When the app offers your ride, accept it right away. The app notifies you before every ride. If you don't accept, dispatch has to call you — too many calls and you lose rides.
2. **Arrive on time.** Being early is on time. If you might be late, call dispatch the moment you know — never hope it works out.
3. **Straight there, straight home.** Pick up, drive the route, drop off. No stops — not for gas, not for coffee, not for errands. Fuel up before your route.
4. **No eating or drinking in the car.** Not you, not the child.
5. **No detours.** Follow the route the app gives you. If the road is blocked, call dispatch.
6. **Never speed.** The app tracks your speed on every ride. One ticket costs more than you earn in a week.

## Module 3 — The app pays you (this is the one drivers skip — don't)
The partner only pays for rides it can verify. That means, on every single ride:
- **Camera working and on.** A ride with no camera footage can be taken back out of your pay.
- **Start the ride in the app when you start. End it when you end.** The app's location record is the proof you did the ride.
- **Be inside the pickup and dropoff zones when you tap.** Tapping from the wrong place looks like a fake ride.
If the app misbehaves, screenshot it and tell dispatch the same morning. A reported problem protects your pay; a silent one doesn't.

## Module 4 — The children
- Greet the child by name; same seat every day if the child prefers it. Routine is comfort.
- If a child has a hard moment (crying, shouting, won't stay seated): **pull over somewhere safe and call dispatch.** You never discipline, never grab, never argue. Dispatch brings in the school or the parent.
- Never leave a child alone in the vehicle. Never drop a child anywhere but the exact stop, to the expected adult where one is required.
- What happens in the car stays private. No photos of children, no posts, no stories.

## Module 5 — If something goes wrong
- **Accident:** children safe first, 911 if anyone is hurt, then call dispatch immediately — before your family, before photos of the car. You'll write down what happened within 24 hours; dispatch will help you.
- **You're sick / can't drive:** tell dispatch the night before if you can, or the second you know. An early callout is respected; a silent no-show can end the contract.
- **Running late:** call dispatch as soon as late looks possible.

## Module 6 — Your vehicle and paperwork
Registration, insurance, and the annual mechanic certification must stay current — an expired document means you cannot drive that day, by contract, no exceptions. Z-Pay reminds you 30 days before anything expires. Handle it that week, not the last day.

## Quiz (pass = 8 of 10, retake allowed after re-reading)
1. The app offers you tomorrow's ride. When do you accept it? *(Immediately.)*
2. You're low on gas mid-route with the child in the car. What do you do? *(Nothing mid-route — fueling happens before the route. Road blocked/emergency → call dispatch.)*
3. Your camera isn't working this morning. What do you do? *(Screenshot/report to dispatch the same morning, before the ride.)*
4. Why do you tap start/end inside the pickup and dropoff zones? *(That's the proof the ride happened — it's how you get paid.)*
5. A child starts screaming and unbuckles mid-ride. What do you do? *(Pull over safely, call dispatch. Never discipline or grab.)*
6. You wake up with a fever at 5am. Ride at 6:40. What do you do? *(Call dispatch immediately — the second you know.)*
7. There's an accident. Nobody is hurt. Who do you call first? *(Dispatch, immediately.)*
8. Can you stop at your cousin's house for two minutes on the way home from dropoff — with no child in the car? *(After dropoff with no child, the route is done; during any route leg with a child: never.)*
9. Your registration expires next month. Z-Pay reminded you. When do you renew? *(That week — an expired document = no driving, by contract.)*
10. A parent asks you to drop the child at the park instead of home. What do you do? *(No — exact stop only. Route changes come from dispatch, never from a verbal ask.)*
