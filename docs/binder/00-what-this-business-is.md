# What This Business Is — One Page

> Franchise Binder §1 · drafted 2026-07-22 · reader: anyone who has to run this without Malik or Zubeda in the room.

## The model, in four sentences
Two partner companies hand Maz Services school-transportation runs: **FirstAlt** (billed through Acumen International, sends a weekly Excel) and **EverDriven** (Maz tenant, sends PDFs). The partners hold the school-district contracts; we never touch districts — we supply the driver pool. Revenue is what the partner pays per ride; cost is what we pay the driver; the spread is the margin. Everything else in this binder is operations around that spread.

## The weekly rhythm
- **Monday (payroll day):** partner file lands → upload to Z-Pay → resolve any unpriced rides → review payroll → approve → email stubs → Paychex submission → drivers get direct deposit around Thursday. Full steps: §2 Payroll Runbook.
- **Every school morning (5:30–8:00am):** confirm every driver with rides is awake and has accepted in the partner app. The system watches automatically and only shows you exceptions. Full steps: §3 Dispatcher Runbook.
- **When a deposit from FirstAlt/EverDriven hits the bank:** record it in Z-Pay the same day. If it's short, we have **14 days** to dispute in writing or the money is legally gone. Full steps: §2 Payroll Runbook, reconciliation section.

## The seasons
- **School year (Sep–Jun):** ~44–48 FirstAlt drivers, batches of $17–24k.
- **Summer (ESY):** ~12 drivers, ~$2–3k batches. EverDriven mostly dormant. **The summer dip is by design** — do not panic about it and do not over-engineer around it.
- Route numbers reset at season boundaries. Prices follow **distance**, not the route name.

## The people
- **Operator (currently Zubeda):** runs payroll and dispatch through the Z-Pay web UI. No terminal, no code.
- **Owner (Malik):** reads the numbers weekly, makes the take/pass call on new rides, handles partner relationships.
- **Drivers:** 1099 contractors, largely Amharic/Arabic-speaking. They own and maintain their own vehicles.
- **Partner contact:** Brandon (FirstStudent) — new rides arrive as his emails.

## The rules that protect the money
1. **Nothing ships or changes near payroll:** deploy freeze Sunday noon → Tuesday 9am.
2. **Paychex has TWO tenants** (Acumen International for FA, Maz Services for ED) under one login — always verify which tenant is on screen before submitting.
3. **App discipline is revenue:** FirstAlt can claw back pay for trips with missing camera, geofence never entered, or app misuse (contract §6c). Drivers follow the app rules or the ride wasn't paid.
4. **Insurance/registration lapse = instant termination risk** (contract §9a). Compliance renewals are annual — the expiry tracker nags before anything lapses.
5. **Never message drivers in bulk without owner approval.**

## What "running well" looks like
Rides completed with zero calls needed · payroll matches Paychex to the penny · deposits match batches · no compliance item inside its 30-day expiry window · the operator's total attention ≤30 min/day. The owner dashboard (§7) reports exactly these.
