# Insurance Verification — Questions for the Broker/Lawyer Call

**Purpose:** This is a script. Read it close to verbatim on ONE call with a commercial insurance broker or transportation attorney. Every question is self-contained — you don't need to explain the whole business first, just read the 1-line context before each one.

**Why this call matters:** Right now Z-Pay's operating assumption is "insurance is believed to sit with partners/drivers" — nobody has verified that with a professional. Both partner contracts have language that shifts real liability onto Acumen/Maz. If there's a gap, it's the kind of gap that ends the business in one bad accident, not the kind that costs a little extra premium. Treat this call as pass/fail, not optional due-diligence.

**Two-LLC context the broker needs upfront:** Acumen International (FirstAlt/FirstStudent work) and Maz Services (EverDriven work) are separate, family-owned LLCs. Drivers are 1099 contractors who own/maintain/register their own vehicles. ~44-48 drivers in-season, ~12 in summer (ESY).

---

## Topic 1 — Primary Liability Gap (FirstAlt / Acumen)

**Context:** The FirstAlt contract (TPA, June 2026) names Acumen International as the PRIMARY insurer-responsible party. FirstAlt's own auto policy is EXPLICITLY EXCESS ONLY — meaning it only pays after Acumen's coverage is exhausted, and it's deniable if Acumen isn't in compliance.

1. "Our contract says we — the transportation company, not the school-bus partner — are the PRIMARY party responsible for liability insurance, and the partner's policy only kicks in as excess coverage above ours. Does Acumen currently have ANY qualifying primary commercial auto/general liability policy in place, or are we currently operating with no primary coverage at all — meaning the partner's 'excess' policy has nothing to sit on top of?"
2. "If we have no primary policy today, what's the minimum policy structure (type, carrier class, coverage triggers) that would actually satisfy 'primary insurer-responsible party' language in a contract like this?"
3. "The contract's insurance acknowledgment section is deniable 'on non-compliance' — what specific compliance conditions (vehicle registration, driver licensing, certificate filing cadence) would void our coverage or the partner's excess layer, and how do we build a checklist so we never trip one accidentally?"

## Topic 2 — Passenger Coverage Adequacy

**Context:** FirstAlt's excess auto policy caps at $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident / $50,000 property damage, and only applies during a "passenger-in-vehicle" window (i.e., not before pickup or after drop-off). We transport school-age children, some special-needs.

4. "Is $100k/$300k/$50k an adequate limit for special-needs student transportation in Washington State, given the severity ceiling on injury claims involving minors? What do comparable operators typically carry?"
5. "What exactly falls OUTSIDE the 'passenger-in-vehicle' window — is a child injured walking to the car, or after being dropped off but still on school property, covered by anything at all, or is that a total gap?"
6. "If a claim exceeds these caps, does liability flow to Acumen/Maz as the LLCs, to me personally as owner, or to the individual driver? What corporate structure or umbrella policy would actually stop that from reaching personal assets?"
7. "Given the contract's ONE-WAY indemnity clause — Acumen indemnifies FirstAlt for everything including wage/workers'-comp claims, while FirstAlt's liability is capped at trailing-12-months payments — what insurance product specifically covers that indemnity obligation, separate from ordinary auto/general liability?"

## Topic 3 — WA L&I (Workers' Compensation) for 1099 Contractors

**Context:** The FirstAlt contract (§13i) requires Acumen to carry "adequate Workers' Compensation or related insurance." All our drivers are classified as 1099 independent contractors, not W-2 employees. This is our single biggest open legal question — we genuinely don't know the answer.

8. "Washington State's L&I (Labor & Industries) has specific, sometimes aggressive tests for whether a '1099 contractor' is actually an employee for workers'-comp purposes, especially in transportation. Based on how our drivers actually work — they use our routes, our per-ride rates set by us, ride-acceptance tracked via a partner app, and can be removed from routes at our discretion — would WA L&I likely reclassify them as employees requiring mandatory L&I coverage?"
9. "If reclassification risk is real, what would mandatory L&I coverage cost per driver, and is there a way to restructure the relationship (true independent-contractor safe harbor) that would hold up under a WA L&I audit — not just have us describe them as 1099 on paper?"
10. "Separately from L&I: does 'Workers' Compensation or related insurance' in the FirstAlt contract mean we need a policy even if drivers are legitimately independent contractors — i.e., is there a contractual obligation here that's broader than the legal minimum?"
11. "What's the actual audit/enforcement exposure — back-premiums, penalties, personal liability for LLC members — if L&I later determines our drivers should have been covered and weren't?"

## Topic 4 — Physical Damage on Driver-Owned Vehicles

**Context:** Drivers use their own personal vehicles. EverDriven's WUD ("While Under Dispatch") program deducts $2.00 per trip from driver pay for group commercial auto LIABILITY coverage via GW Purchasing Group — but that coverage explicitly EXCLUDES physical damage to the vehicle itself. FirstAlt work has no equivalent program we're aware of.

12. "If a driver's personal vehicle is damaged or totaled in an accident during a dispatched ride, who is actually financially responsible for that vehicle today — the driver's personal auto policy (which may exclude commercial use), Acumen/Maz, or nobody?"
13. "Most personal auto policies have a commercial-use exclusion that voids coverage entirely once a vehicle is used for paid transport. Are our drivers' personal policies at risk of denying ALL claims — not just physical damage, but liability too — because they're doing paid work in a personal vehicle?"
14. "Is there an affordable product (hired/non-owned auto physical damage, or a driver-side commercial auto rider) that would close this gap, and roughly what does it cost per vehicle or per driver?"
15. "For FirstAlt work specifically — is there ANY physical-damage coverage in place today, or is this gap effectively the same on both partner sides?"

## Topic 5 — Certificate & Compliance Requirements

**Context:** The FirstAlt contract (§4c) requires ANNUAL third-party mechanic certification per registered vehicle. §9a allows INSTANT contract termination on insurance lapse, unregistered vehicle, or unregistered driver — no cure period. We need to know if our current fleet actually meets this, and what ongoing tracking a broker would recommend.

16. "What certificates of insurance, in what form and how often, do we need to be able to produce on demand for FirstAlt and for EverDriven to avoid an instant-termination trigger under §9a?"
17. "Is annual third-party mechanic certification per vehicle (§4c) something a standard commercial auto policy requires as a precondition of coverage, or is that purely a contractual requirement from FirstAlt separate from insurance — i.e., could a vehicle be insured but still in breach of contract if it lacks the mechanic cert?"
18. "What's a reasonable compliance calendar/cadence (registration renewal, mechanic cert renewal, COI renewal) you'd recommend so we never get caught by an expired document mid-contract?"
19. "Do you recommend we get everything above in writing from FirstAlt/EverDriven directly (a written confirmation of exactly what's covered on their side), or is verbal/contract-language confirmation sufficient?"
